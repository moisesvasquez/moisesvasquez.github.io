About Dr. Peter A. Barone, Esq.

Founder

Legal and Use of Force Expert and a Criminologist. An experienced courtroom witness who has testified in and conducted thousands of legal proceedings including depositions, hearings, and trials. He has instructed thousands of law enforcement officers all over the country since 1997 as a National Lecturer for HIDTA; Office of the Florida Attorney General and Barone Consulting. He has been in Law Enforcement since 1975 and investigated major cases such as the Miami River Cops; the FBI Shootings and several murders of law enforcement officers. He worked as a Hostage Negotiator, has written, assessed and participated in several Active Shooter Scenarios with the Sheriffâ€™s Office and APC Consultants.Has attended over 230 certification courses from law enforcement, law, and military. He holds Active Shooter, Use of Force, OC, ASP, Handcuffing, Impact Weapon, Defensive Driving, Police Legal Issues, First Aid, CPR, Explosive Ordinance and Chemical Weapons certifications. He is a licensed practicing attorney since 1992 and served as a legal Police Legal Advisor, Major Crimes Felony Prosecutor, and a Certified Mediator. He was a certified Counterintelligence Agent with the DOD; served in the Middle East. He has been a university professor since 1996; Coordinator of two programs and currently the Chair of the Criminal Justice Management and Pre-Law Programs. He has been a national lecturer, a military and police instructor since 1983. He is on the Fatality Review and Domestic Fatality Review Boards for the 10th Judicial Circuit Florida. He conducts seminars for Attorneys and Physicians approved for CLE and CME credits. He has a Ph.D. in Public Safety and Criminal Justice; an LL.M degree (post doctorate) in International Law; a J.D. in Law; an M.S.M. in Management; a B.S. in Human Services Criminal Justice, and an A.S, a degree in Law Enforcement. He has published a book; chapters in books and several articles and has been invited and presented at several National Conferences. His major area of research is to examine 6,000 Officer Involved Shootings from police agencies in all 50 states.
